services

What is light?

Light is a powerful medium.

It is intangible, invisible, ethereal and ever-changing, yet it can transform and reveal spaces, provide ambience, simulate textures, trigger emotions and influence behaviour.

As Architectural Lighting Designers, we use both light and darkness to enhance the spatial experience and visual environment. Despite common beliefs, we don't see light as a decorative add-on, but as a fundamental tool to reveal the architectural design.

How do we work with light?

We get involved at the first stages of the design process to carefully understand design briefs, client needs, budget constraints and programmes. Working closely with architects and designers, we develop narratives that define the core character of the architectural design and create unique atmospheres.

We remain independent yet collaborate with various lighting manufacturers and suppliers worldwide to provide an unbiased approach. Through the use of lighting software, samples and mock-ups, we carefully select luminaires by evaluating cost, quality and performance to successfully deliver our lighting schemes.

How do we collaborate?

We follow the typical RIBA Plan of Work which allows close collaboration with design teams whilst simultaneously providing creative freedom.

Stage 1: Preparation and Brief
  1. Attend Briefing meetings.
  2. Assist with development of Lighting Brief.
  3. Assist with the preparation of Lighting Budget.
  4. Feasibility or design reports (if applicable).
Stage 2: Concept Design
  1. Attend Design Team Meetings and Workshops.
  2. Carry out of conceptual lighting design studies.
  3. Prepare presentation materials to communicate design intent.
  4. Lighting Design budget estimate.
Stage 3: Schematic Design
  1. Attend Design Team Meetings and Workshops.
  2. Carry out of further development of lighting design.
  3. Preliminary Lighting Layouts (CAD / PDF).
  4. Preliminary Lighting Equipment Schedule.
  5. Preliminary Lighting Details (where applicable).
  6. Preliminary Control Philosophy.
  7. Preliminary Logical Control Channel Schedule.
  8. Carry out typical Preliminary Lighting Calculations (where applicable).
  9. Lighting Design cost report (+ or - 25%).
Stage 4: Detail Design
  1. Attend Design Team Meetings / Workshops / Mock-ups.
  2. Assist others with the co-ordination of the lighting scheme.
  3. Final Lighting Layouts (CAD / PDF).
  4. Final Lighting Equipment Specification.
  5. Final Lighting Details (where applicable).
  6. Final Logical Control Channel Schedule.
  7. Final Pre-Programming Schedule.
  8. Comment on the final coordinated construction drawings.
Stage 5: Construction
  1. Attend Contract Meetings / Reviews / Mock-ups.
  2. Attend site visit with design teams.
  3. Review and Comment on ongoing lighting installation.
  4. Comment on Contractor’s drawings, details and specifications.
  5. Respond to RFI’s and Design Change Orders.
Stage 6: Handover and Closeout
  1. Attend Contract Meetings / Programming / Focussing / Snagging.
  2. Direct the focusing of installed lighting equipment where needed.
  3. Direct the programming of installed lighting equipment where needed.
  4. Review final lighting installation.

about

Youmna Abdallah is an award-winning Architectural Lighting Designer holding extensive experience in the Lighting Design Industry between London, Zürich and Beirut.

Since 2012, Youmna has been involved in illuminating prominent buildings around the world while working at Lighting Design practices Speirs + Major, MBLD and Lichtkompetenz. Her work includes projects of wide-ranging types and scales through collaborations with Foster + Partners, Santiago Calatrava, Zaha Hadid Architects, Studio Fuksas, Perkins + Will and Universal Design Studio amongst others.

Youmna's passion for light has led her to win multiple awards by CIBSE’s Society of Light and Lighting in London. Amongst them are the Young Lighter of the Year 2015 which she won for advocating the importance of quality lighting in impoverished areas of the world. She has also been a guest lecturer at several lighting events in London to raise awareness about light poverty and Kerozen eradication in the African continent.

In 2019, Youmna was appointed as Creative Director of Luxlab in Beirut, after which she moved on to establishing her independent Lighting Design practice in 2020. She is currently working on diverse projects in Europe, Middle-East and South-East Asia.

Youmna holds an Interior Design Masters Degree from Scuola Politecnica Di Design in Milan, and an Interior Architecture Bachelor Degree from Académie Libanaise des Beaux-Arts in Beirut where she graduated in 2011.
